# Records Retention Schedule

## Purpose

This records retention schedule defines the retention periods for different types of records maintained by {{CompanyName}}, in compliance with the National Archives and Records Service of South Africa (NARSA) Act No. 43 of 1996 and related regulations.

## Scope

This schedule applies to all records created, received, or maintained by {{CompanyName}}, including:

- Administrative records
- Financial records
- Human resources records
- Legal records
- Operational records
- Strategic records

## Retention Principles

1. Records must be retained for the minimum periods specified in this schedule
2. Records may be retained longer if required for business or legal purposes
3. Records must be destroyed in accordance with approved disposal procedures
4. Records of permanent value must be transferred to National Archives
5. Electronic records must be retained in accordance with the same retention periods as paper records

## Disposal Procedures

### Authorization

1. Records may only be disposed of after the retention period has expired
2. Disposal must be authorized by the Records Manager
3. Disposal of permanent records requires approval from National Archives
4. Disposal must be documented in the disposal register

### Disposal Methods

- **Paper Records:** Secure shredding or incineration
- **Electronic Records:** Secure deletion and overwriting
- **Physical Media:** Secure destruction or degaussing

### Disposal Register

All disposals must be recorded in the disposal register, including:

- Record type and description
- Date of disposal
- Quantity disposed
- Disposal method
- Authorization details

## Transfer to National Archives

Records of permanent value must be transferred to National Archives:

- Transfer must be approved by National Archives
- Transfer must be documented
- Records must be properly packaged and labeled
- Transfer list must be maintained

## Review and Updates

This retention schedule must be reviewed annually and updated as necessary to reflect:

- Changes in legislation
- Changes in business requirements
- Changes in record types
- Feedback from National Archives

## Responsibilities

### Records Manager
- Maintenance of this retention schedule
- Authorization of record disposals
- Coordination of transfers to National Archives

### Department Heads
- Ensuring records are retained according to this schedule
- Identifying records for disposal
- Submitting records for disposal authorization

### All Employees
- Following retention requirements
- Not disposing of records without authorization
- Reporting records that may require retention
