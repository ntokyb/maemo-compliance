# Training and Competence Procedure

## Purpose

This procedure establishes the requirements for:

- Determining competence requirements for personnel
- Ensuring personnel are competent based on education, training, and experience
- Providing training where needed
- Evaluating the effectiveness of training
- Maintaining training records

## Scope

This procedure applies to all personnel performing work that affects quality, including:

- Management personnel
- Process owners and operators
- Quality personnel
- Internal auditors
- Customer-facing personnel

## Responsibilities

### Human Resources / Department Heads
- Determining competence requirements
- Identifying training needs
- Planning and organizing training
- Maintaining training records

### Quality Manager
- Determining competence requirements for quality-related roles
- Coordinating quality training
- Verifying training effectiveness

### Supervisors
- Identifying training needs for their teams
- Recommending training
- Monitoring competence
- Providing on-the-job training

### Personnel
- Participating in required training
- Maintaining their own competence
- Reporting training needs

## Procedure

### 4.1 Competence Requirements

1. Competence requirements are determined for each role based on:
   - Job description and responsibilities
   - Process requirements
   - Quality objectives
   - Legal and regulatory requirements
   - Customer requirements

2. Competence requirements specify:
   - Education level
   - Training requirements
   - Experience level
   - Skills and knowledge
   - Certifications (where applicable)

3. Competence requirements are documented in:
   - Job descriptions
   - Competence matrices
   - Training plans

### 4.2 Competence Assessment

1. Personnel competence is assessed against requirements:
   - During recruitment
   - During performance reviews
   - When roles change
   - When processes change

2. Assessment methods include:
   - Review of qualifications and experience
   - Skills testing
   - Observation of work performance
   - Interview or discussion
   - Certification verification

3. Competence gaps are identified and documented

### 4.3 Training Needs Identification

Training needs are identified through:

- Competence assessments
- Process changes
- New equipment or technology
- Nonconformities and corrective actions
- Customer feedback
- Management review
- Performance reviews

### 4.4 Training Planning

1. Annual training plan is developed covering:
   - Required training for each role
   - Mandatory training (safety, quality, compliance)
   - Skills development training
   - Refresher training

2. Training plan includes:
   - Training topics
   - Target audience
   - Training method (internal, external, on-the-job)
   - Schedule
   - Budget

### 4.5 Training Delivery

1. Training is delivered through:
   - Internal training sessions
   - External training courses
   - On-the-job training
   - E-learning
   - Workshops and seminars

2. Training is provided by:
   - Qualified internal trainers
   - External training providers
   - Supervisors and experienced personnel

3. Training content is:
   - Relevant to job requirements
   - Up-to-date and accurate
   - Documented and controlled

### 4.6 Training Evaluation

1. Training effectiveness is evaluated through:
   - Post-training assessment (tests, quizzes)
   - Observation of work performance
   - Feedback from trainees
   - Feedback from supervisors
   - Impact on quality objectives

2. Evaluation methods:
   - Immediate: Knowledge test, feedback form
   - Short-term: Performance observation (within 30 days)
   - Long-term: Impact on quality indicators (within 6 months)

3. Evaluation results are:
   - Documented
   - Reviewed
   - Used to improve training

### 4.7 Training Records

Training records are maintained for each person including:

- Person's name and role
- Training topics and dates
- Training provider
- Training duration
- Assessment results
- Certificates or qualifications
- Competence verification

### 4.8 Awareness

Personnel are made aware of:

- Quality policy and objectives
- Their contribution to quality
- Consequences of nonconformity
- Benefits of improved performance
- Their role in the QMS

## Records

The following records are maintained:

- Competence requirements
- Competence assessment records
- Training plans
- Training records
- Training evaluation records
- Certificates and qualifications
