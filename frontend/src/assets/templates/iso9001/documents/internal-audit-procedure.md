# Internal Audit Procedure

## Purpose

This procedure establishes the requirements for planning, conducting, and reporting internal audits of the Quality Management System (QMS) to:

- Verify conformity to ISO 9001:2015 requirements
- Verify conformity to QMS requirements
- Identify opportunities for improvement
- Ensure the QMS is effectively implemented and maintained

## Scope

This procedure applies to internal audits of:

- All QMS processes
- All departments and functions
- All locations (where applicable)
- External provider processes (where applicable)

## Responsibilities

### Quality Manager
- Overall coordination of internal audit program
- Appointing audit team leaders
- Reviewing audit reports
- Ensuring follow-up on audit findings

### Audit Team Leader
- Planning and conducting audits
- Managing the audit team
- Preparing audit reports
- Verifying corrective actions

### Auditors
- Conducting audit activities
- Collecting objective evidence
- Documenting audit findings
- Reporting to audit team leader

### Auditees
- Providing access to processes and records
- Assisting auditors as required
- Implementing corrective actions for audit findings

## Procedure

### 4.1 Audit Planning

1. Annual audit program is developed covering:
   - All QMS processes
   - All departments
   - Frequency based on process importance and performance
   - Schedule for the year

2. For each audit:
   - Audit scope and objectives are defined
   - Audit team is appointed
   - Audit plan is prepared
   - Auditees are notified

### 4.2 Audit Team Selection

1. Auditors are selected based on:
   - Competence and training
   - Independence from audited area
   - Knowledge of ISO 9001:2015
   - Knowledge of audited processes

2. Auditors must be:
   - Trained in audit techniques
   - Objective and impartial
   - Free from conflicts of interest

### 4.3 Audit Preparation

1. Audit team reviews:
   - Previous audit reports
   - Process documentation
   - Quality objectives and performance data
   - Nonconformities and corrective actions

2. Audit checklist is prepared (if used)
3. Opening meeting is scheduled

### 4.4 Conducting the Audit

1. Opening meeting is held with auditees:
   - Audit scope and objectives are confirmed
   - Audit plan is reviewed
   - Audit process is explained

2. Audit activities:
   - Interviews with personnel
   - Review of documents and records
   - Observation of processes
   - Collection of objective evidence

3. Audit findings are documented:
   - Conformities
   - Nonconformities (with evidence)
   - Opportunities for improvement

### 4.5 Audit Reporting

1. Audit report is prepared including:
   - Audit scope and objectives
   - Audit team members
   - Audit dates and locations
   - Summary of audit activities
   - Audit findings (conformities and nonconformities)
   - Opportunities for improvement
   - Conclusions

2. Closing meeting is held:
   - Findings are presented
   - Nonconformities are discussed
   - Corrective action requirements are explained

3. Audit report is distributed to:
   - Quality Manager
   - Process owners
   - Top management (for management review)

### 4.6 Corrective Action

1. Auditees develop corrective action plans for nonconformities
2. Plans are reviewed and approved by Quality Manager
3. Corrective actions are implemented
4. Effectiveness is verified through follow-up audit or review

### 4.7 Follow-up Audit

1. Follow-up audit is conducted to verify:
   - Corrective actions have been implemented
   - Corrective actions are effective
   - Nonconformities have been resolved

2. Results are documented
3. Audit is closed when all findings are resolved

## Records

The following records are maintained:

- Annual audit program
- Audit plans
- Audit reports
- Audit findings and nonconformities
- Corrective action plans
- Follow-up audit reports
- Auditor training records
