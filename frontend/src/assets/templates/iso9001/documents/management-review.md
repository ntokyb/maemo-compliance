# Management Review Procedure

## Purpose

This procedure establishes the requirements for conducting management reviews to:

- Ensure the continuing suitability, adequacy, and effectiveness of the Quality Management System
- Evaluate opportunities for improvement
- Assess the need for changes to the QMS
- Review quality objectives and performance
- Make decisions on resource allocation

## Scope

This procedure applies to management reviews conducted by top management of {{CompanyName}}.

## Responsibilities

### Top Management
- Conducting management reviews
- Making decisions on QMS changes
- Allocating resources
- Approving quality objectives

### Quality Manager
- Preparing management review inputs
- Coordinating management review meetings
- Documenting management review outputs
- Following up on management review decisions

### Process Owners
- Providing input on process performance
- Attending management review meetings
- Implementing management review decisions

## Procedure

### 4.1 Review Frequency

1. Management reviews are conducted at planned intervals:
   - At least annually
   - More frequently if required by:
     - Significant changes in the organisation
     - Performance issues
     - Customer complaints
     - External audit findings

2. Additional reviews may be scheduled as needed

### 4.2 Review Inputs

The following inputs are prepared for each management review:

1. **Status of actions from previous reviews**
   - Actions taken
   - Results achieved
   - Outstanding actions

2. **Changes in external and internal issues**
   - Market conditions
   - Regulatory changes
   - Organisational changes
   - Technology changes

3. **Information on QMS performance**
   - Quality objectives achievement
   - Process performance indicators
   - Customer satisfaction and feedback
   - Nonconformities and corrective actions
   - Internal audit results
   - External audit results

4. **Monitoring and measurement results**
   - Product/service conformity
   - Process performance data
   - Customer satisfaction surveys
   - Supplier performance

5. **Audit results**
   - Internal audit reports
   - External audit reports
   - Follow-up actions

6. **Customer feedback**
   - Complaints and compliments
   - Customer satisfaction surveys
   - Service level agreements

7. **Performance of external providers**
   - Supplier evaluations
   - Nonconformities
   - Corrective actions

8. **Resource adequacy**
   - Personnel
   - Infrastructure
   - Work environment
   - Knowledge

9. **Effectiveness of actions taken**
   - Corrective actions
   - Preventive actions
   - Improvement initiatives

10. **Risks and opportunities**
    - Risk register updates
    - Opportunity assessments
    - Risk mitigation actions

### 4.3 Review Meeting

1. Management review meeting is conducted with:
   - Top management (required)
   - Quality Manager (required)
   - Process owners (as required)
   - Other relevant personnel (as required)

2. Review inputs are presented and discussed
3. Decisions are made on:
   - QMS effectiveness
   - Opportunities for improvement
   - Changes to the QMS
   - Resource needs
   - Quality objectives

### 4.4 Review Outputs

Management review outputs include:

1. **Decisions and actions related to:**
   - Improvements to products and services
   - Improvements to processes
   - Resource needs
   - Quality objectives

2. **Opportunities for improvement**
   - Process improvements
   - System improvements
   - Innovation opportunities

3. **Any need for changes to the QMS**
   - Procedure updates
   - Process changes
   - Policy changes

### 4.5 Review Records

1. Management review is documented with:
   - Date and attendees
   - Review inputs presented
   - Discussions and decisions
   - Actions assigned
   - Target dates for actions

2. Management review minutes are prepared
3. Minutes are distributed to attendees
4. Records are maintained

### 4.6 Follow-up

1. Actions from management review are:
   - Assigned to responsible persons
   - Scheduled with target dates
   - Monitored for completion
   - Reviewed at next management review

2. Quality Manager coordinates follow-up
3. Progress is reported to top management

## Records

The following records are maintained:

- Management review agenda
- Management review minutes
- Management review inputs
- Management review outputs
- Action item tracking
- Follow-up reports
