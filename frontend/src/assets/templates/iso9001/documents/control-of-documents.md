# Control of Documents Procedure

## Purpose

This procedure establishes the requirements for controlling documents to ensure that:

- Documents are identified, reviewed, approved, and maintained
- Current versions of documents are available at points of use
- Documents are legible and readily identifiable
- Obsolete documents are prevented from unintended use
- Changes to documents are identified and controlled

## Scope

This procedure applies to all documents that form part of the quality management system at {{CompanyName}}, including:

- Quality manual
- Procedures and work instructions
- Forms and templates
- External documents (standards, regulations, customer specifications)
- Records (controlled copies of completed documents)

## Responsibilities

### Quality Manager
- Overall responsibility for document control
- Approval of quality manual and procedures
- Maintenance of the master document register

### Document Owner
- Review and update of assigned documents
- Ensuring documents reflect current practices
- Submitting documents for review and approval

### All Employees
- Using current versions of documents
- Reporting obsolete or incorrect documents
- Following document control procedures

## Procedure

### 4.1 Document Creation

1. Document owner creates draft document using approved template
2. Document is assigned a unique document number and revision number
3. Document includes:
   - Document title
   - Document number
   - Revision number and date
   - Approval signatures
   - Distribution list

### 4.2 Document Review

1. Document owner submits draft for review
2. Reviewers check for:
   - Accuracy and completeness
   - Compliance with ISO 9001 requirements
   - Clarity and usability
   - Consistency with other documents

### 4.3 Document Approval

1. Approved reviewers sign document approval form
2. Quality Manager approves quality manual and procedures
3. Department heads approve work instructions and forms
4. Approval date is recorded

### 4.4 Document Distribution

1. Approved documents are distributed according to distribution list
2. Recipients acknowledge receipt
3. Master copy is maintained in controlled location
4. Electronic copies are stored in secure document management system

### 4.5 Document Changes

1. Change requests are submitted to document owner
2. Changes are reviewed and approved following same process as new documents
3. Revision number is incremented
4. Changed documents are redistributed to all recipients
5. Obsolete versions are removed from points of use

### 4.6 Obsolete Documents

1. Obsolete documents are clearly marked as "OBSOLETE"
2. Obsolete documents are removed from active use
3. Master copies are archived for retention period
4. Electronic copies are marked as superseded

## Records

The following records are maintained:

- Document register (master list of all controlled documents)
- Document approval forms
- Document distribution records
- Document change requests
- Obsolete document archive
