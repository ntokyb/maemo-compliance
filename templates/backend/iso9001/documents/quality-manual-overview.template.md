# Quality Manual Overview

## Purpose

This Quality Manual provides an overview of the Quality Management System (QMS) implemented at {{CompanyName}} to demonstrate compliance with ISO 9001:2015 requirements. It describes the structure of the QMS, key processes, and how the organisation ensures quality in its products and services.

## Scope

This manual applies to all activities, processes, and personnel within {{CompanyName}} that affect the quality of products and services delivered to customers. The QMS covers:

- Quality planning and objectives
- Process management and control
- Resource management
- Customer focus and satisfaction
- Continuous improvement

## Quality Policy

{{CompanyName}} is committed to:

- Meeting customer requirements and exceeding expectations
- Complying with applicable legal and regulatory requirements
- Continually improving the effectiveness of the QMS
- Providing adequate resources to support quality objectives
- Engaging and developing personnel to achieve quality goals

## Quality Management System Structure

### Process Approach

The QMS is based on a process approach, where:

- Processes are identified, defined, and documented
- Process interactions are mapped and understood
- Process performance is monitored and measured
- Process improvements are implemented based on data and analysis

### Key Processes

1. **Management Processes**
   - Strategic planning
   - Management review
   - Resource allocation
   - Risk and opportunity management

2. **Core Business Processes**
   - Product/service delivery
   - Customer relationship management
   - Design and development (where applicable)
   - Procurement and supply chain management

3. **Support Processes**
   - Document and record control
   - Training and competence
   - Internal audit
   - Nonconformity and corrective action
   - Continual improvement

## ISO 9001:2015 Requirements

The QMS addresses all requirements of ISO 9001:2015 through:

- **Context of the Organisation**: Understanding internal and external factors, interested parties, and scope
- **Leadership**: Top management commitment and quality policy
- **Planning**: Quality objectives and risk-based thinking
- **Support**: Resources, competence, awareness, communication, and documented information
- **Operation**: Planning, control, design, external providers, production/service provision, release, and nonconforming outputs
- **Performance Evaluation**: Monitoring, measurement, analysis, evaluation, internal audit, and management review
- **Improvement**: Nonconformity, corrective action, and continual improvement

## Documented Information

The QMS includes documented information as required by ISO 9001:2015:

- Quality Manual (this document)
- Procedures and work instructions
- Forms and templates
- Quality records
- External documents (standards, regulations, customer specifications)

## Responsibilities

### Top Management

- Establishing and maintaining the quality policy
- Ensuring quality objectives are established
- Providing resources
- Promoting continual improvement
- Conducting management reviews

### Quality Manager

- Overall coordination of the QMS
- Ensuring compliance with ISO 9001:2015
- Coordinating internal audits
- Managing corrective actions
- Reporting on QMS performance

### Process Owners

- Managing assigned processes
- Ensuring process effectiveness
- Identifying and implementing improvements
- Maintaining process documentation

## Continual Improvement

{{CompanyName}} is committed to continual improvement through:

- Regular monitoring and measurement of processes
- Analysis of data and trends
- Management review of QMS performance
- Corrective and preventive actions
- Innovation and process optimization
