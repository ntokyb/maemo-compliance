# Control of Records Procedure

## Purpose

This procedure establishes the requirements for controlling records to ensure that:

- Records provide evidence of conformity to requirements
- Records demonstrate the effective operation of the Quality Management System
- Records are legible, identifiable, and retrievable
- Records are protected from loss, deterioration, or unauthorized access
- Records are retained and disposed of in accordance with legal and business requirements

## Scope

This procedure applies to all quality records created, maintained, or stored by {{CompanyName}}, including:

- Quality audit records
- Management review records
- Training records
- Nonconformity and corrective action records
- Customer feedback and complaints
- Process performance records
- Product/service verification records

## Responsibilities

### Quality Manager
- Overall responsibility for records control
- Establishing retention periods
- Authorizing record disposal
- Maintaining the records register

### Record Owner
- Creating and maintaining assigned records
- Ensuring records are complete and accurate
- Protecting records from unauthorized access
- Submitting records for disposal when retention period expires

### All Personnel
- Creating accurate and complete records
- Storing records in designated locations
- Not altering or destroying records without authorization
- Reporting record issues or concerns

## Procedure

### 4.1 Record Creation

1. Records are created using approved forms or templates
2. Records are completed accurately and legibly
3. Records include:
   - Date of creation
   - Name and signature of person creating the record
   - Relevant identification numbers or references
   - Required information fields

### 4.2 Record Identification

1. Each record is assigned a unique identifier
2. Records are labeled with:
   - Record type
   - Date range or period covered
   - Department or process owner
   - Retention period

### 4.3 Record Storage

1. Records are stored in secure locations:
   - Physical records: Designated filing areas with access controls
   - Electronic records: Secure systems with backup and access controls
2. Storage conditions protect records from:
   - Damage, deterioration, or loss
   - Unauthorized access, alteration, or deletion
   - Fire, water, or environmental hazards
3. Records are organized for easy retrieval

### 4.4 Record Retrieval

1. Authorized personnel may retrieve records as needed
2. Record retrieval is logged (date, person, purpose)
3. Records are returned promptly after use
4. Electronic records access is logged automatically

### 4.5 Record Retention

1. Retention periods are defined in the Records Retention Schedule
2. Retention periods consider:
   - Legal and regulatory requirements
   - Customer requirements
   - Business needs
   - ISO 9001:2015 requirements
3. Records are retained for the minimum retention period
4. Records may be retained longer if required

### 4.6 Record Disposal

1. Records may only be disposed of after the retention period has expired
2. Disposal must be authorized by the Quality Manager
3. Disposal methods:
   - Paper records: Secure shredding or incineration
   - Electronic records: Secure deletion and overwriting
   - Physical media: Secure destruction
4. Disposal is documented in the disposal register

### 4.7 Record Protection

1. Records are protected from:
   - Unauthorized access
   - Alteration or tampering
   - Loss or destruction
   - Environmental damage
2. Access controls are implemented:
   - Physical: Locked storage, access logs
   - Electronic: User authentication, access rights, audit logs
3. Backup copies are maintained for critical records

## Records

The following records are maintained:

- Records register (master list of all quality records)
- Records retention schedule
- Record disposal register
- Record access logs (where applicable)
