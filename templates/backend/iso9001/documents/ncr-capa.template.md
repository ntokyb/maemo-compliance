# Nonconformity and Corrective Action Procedure

## Purpose

This procedure establishes the requirements for:

- Identifying and documenting nonconformities
- Taking immediate action to contain nonconformities
- Investigating root causes of nonconformities
- Implementing corrective actions to prevent recurrence
- Verifying the effectiveness of corrective actions

## Scope

This procedure applies to all nonconformities identified in:

- Products or services
- Processes
- The Quality Management System
- External provider products or services
- Customer complaints

## Responsibilities

### Quality Manager
- Overall coordination of nonconformity and corrective action processes
- Reviewing and approving corrective action plans
- Verifying effectiveness of corrective actions
- Reporting on nonconformities and corrective actions

### Process Owner
- Investigating nonconformities in their processes
- Developing and implementing corrective actions
- Monitoring effectiveness of corrective actions
- Reporting progress to Quality Manager

### All Personnel
- Reporting nonconformities immediately
- Participating in root cause analysis
- Implementing assigned corrective actions
- Following up on corrective actions

## Procedure

### 4.1 Identification of Nonconformities

Nonconformities may be identified through:

- Internal audits
- External audits
- Customer complaints
- Process monitoring and measurement
- Product/service inspection
- Management review
- Employee observations

### 4.2 Immediate Action

1. When a nonconformity is identified:
   - Immediate action is taken to contain the nonconformity
   - Affected products/services are identified and isolated
   - Customers are notified if necessary
   - Nonconformity is documented

2. Immediate actions may include:
   - Stopping production or service delivery
   - Quarantining affected products
   - Recalling products from customers
   - Implementing temporary controls

### 4.3 Nonconformity Documentation

Each nonconformity is documented with:

- Nonconformity description
- Date and location of identification
- Person who identified the nonconformity
- Affected products, services, or processes
- Immediate actions taken
- Severity classification (minor, major, critical)

### 4.4 Root Cause Analysis

1. Root cause analysis is conducted to determine why the nonconformity occurred
2. Analysis methods may include:
   - 5 Whys analysis
   - Fishbone (Ishikawa) diagram
   - Process mapping
   - Data analysis
3. Root cause is documented

### 4.5 Corrective Action Planning

1. Corrective action plan is developed to address the root cause
2. Plan includes:
   - Actions to be taken
   - Responsible person(s)
   - Target completion date
   - Success criteria
3. Plan is reviewed and approved by Quality Manager

### 4.6 Implementation of Corrective Actions

1. Corrective actions are implemented according to the plan
2. Progress is monitored and reported
3. Changes to processes, procedures, or systems are documented
4. Training is provided if required

### 4.7 Verification of Effectiveness

1. Effectiveness of corrective actions is verified:
   - Within 30 days of implementation (or as specified)
   - Through monitoring and measurement
   - Through follow-up audit or review
2. If corrective actions are not effective:
   - Additional analysis is conducted
   - Revised corrective actions are implemented
3. Results are documented

### 4.8 Closure

1. Nonconformity is closed when:
   - Root cause has been addressed
   - Corrective actions have been implemented
   - Effectiveness has been verified
2. Closure is approved by Quality Manager
3. Records are maintained

## Records

The following records are maintained:

- Nonconformity reports
- Root cause analysis records
- Corrective action plans
- Corrective action implementation records
- Effectiveness verification records
- Nonconformity register
